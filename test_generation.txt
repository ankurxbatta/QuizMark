Chapter 1: Statistics Basics

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data.
A population is the entire group being studied.
A sample is a subset of the population.
The mean is the average of all values in a dataset.
The median is the middle value when data is ordered.
The mode is the value that appears most frequently.

The range measures the spread from minimum to maximum.
Variance measures how spread out values are from the mean.
Standard deviation is the square root of variance.

A z-score tells us how many standard deviations a value is from the mean.
The normal distribution is bell-shaped and symmetric around the mean.
